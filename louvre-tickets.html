Buying Louvre tickets in advance is one of the simplest ways to protect your Paris itinerary. The museum recommends time-slot reservations—even for visitors who qualify for free entry—and requires reservations for most visitors from July 1 through August 31, 2026.

Quick recommendation: Choose a guided highlights ticket if this is your first visit and you want the Mona Lisa, Venus de Milo, and Winged Victory with expert context. Choose official timed admission if you prefer exploring independently at the lowest available admission price.

Which Louvre tickets should you buy?

1. Timed general-admission ticket

Best for independent visitors who already know what they want to see. Standard admission covers the Louvre’s permanent collection and temporary exhibitions, plus same-day or next-day entry to the Musée National Eugène-Delacroix.

2. Guided Louvre highlights ticket

Best for a first visit, a shorter Paris stay, or anyone who wants the stories behind the artworks. The featured GetYourGuide option includes pre-reserved museum admission, a licensed English-speaking guide, headsets, and a carefully planned highlights route.

3. Admission plus audio guide

A useful middle ground for travelers who want interpretation without following a group. The official audio guide is currently €6 and is offered in nine languages, subject to availability.

4. Free Louvre ticket

Visitors under 18 and eligible EEA residents or citizens under 26 can enter free with valid proof. Other categories also qualify. A timed reservation is still recommended, and qualifying visitors should carry the required original identification.

Best times to visit the Louvre and avoid queues

The Louvre opens at 9:00 a.m. and closes at 6:00 p.m. on Monday, Thursday, Saturday, and Sunday. Wednesday and Friday have extended hours until 9:00 p.m.; the museum is closed on Tuesday. Last admission is one hour before closing, and galleries begin clearing 30 minutes before closing.

For a calmer experience, look first at the earliest available morning slot or a Wednesday or Friday evening. No time is guaranteed to be quiet, but arriving before your assigned entry time and avoiding the middle of the day can make the visit easier. Remember that every visitor must pass security.

Which Louvre entrance should ticket holders use?

The Pyramid is the main entrance and has a marked line for visitors with timed Louvre tickets. Ticket holders may also use the underground Carrousel entrance or Porte des Lions when open. Porte des Lions requires a ticket, closes earlier, and is closed on Tuesdays. Entrance operations can change, so check the museum’s official entrance map on the day of your visit.

The masterpieces you are expected to see

Mona Lisa

Leonardo da Vinci’s portrait is the Louvre’s most famous work—and often its busiest stop. A guide helps place the painting in its Renaissance setting and explains how events such as its 1911 theft contributed to its extraordinary fame.

Venus de Milo

This ancient Greek sculpture has influenced generations of artists. Beyond the missing arms, its pose, drapery, and uncertain identity give visitors much more to examine.

Winged Victory of Samothrace

Displayed dramatically above the Daru staircase, the Hellenistic figure of Nike appears to stand against sea and wind. Its placement makes it one of the museum’s most striking arrivals.

The medieval Louvre

Before it became a museum—or a royal palace—the Louvre began as a fortress. The underground remains reveal a side of the building that hurried visitors can easily overlook.

Tips for a smoother Louvre visit

  1. Book before your Paris trip when possible. Fixed-time tours can fill, particularly on weekends and during peak seasons.
  2. Read the meeting-point instructions. The current listing uses the Arc de Triomphe du Carrousel outside the museum; do not assume the meeting place is at the pyramid entrance.
  3. Arrive early. Leave a buffer for transport, locating the group, and security procedures.
  4. Wear comfortable shoes. Even a focused route involves considerable walking and standing.
  5. Bring only what you need. Museum security and bag rules apply. Check the official Louvre visitor information shortly before your visit.
  6. Keep your confirmation accessible. Save it on your phone and check whether the activity provider requests any additional document.

Is a Louvre highlights tour worth it?

For many first-time visitors, yes. You are paying for structure and interpretation as much as admission: someone else handles the route while explaining why these objects matter. That can turn a rushed checklist into a coherent introduction to the museum.

If you already know the collection, enjoy navigating independently, or want the lowest-cost entry option, a standard timed museum ticket may suit you better. Compare the live tour price with direct admission and decide how much you value the guided context.
